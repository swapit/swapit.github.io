SwapIt: a Recommender System for Bartering Platforms

Jérémie Rappaz, Maria Vladarean
Julian McAuley, Michele Catasta

PROJECT

Welcome to the SWAPIT Project. We are a team of students and researchers from Ecole Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL) who are passionate about alternative economic models. As we want to know more about the inner workings of barter exchange communities, we spent the past few month analyzing the intricacies of several bartering platforms covering beers, books and video games.

ABSTRACT

Bartering is a popular practice in settings where items are rare or difficult to find, or when users are conscious of price, or wish to be respectful towards the environment. Recommending trades for an online bartering platform has many similarities with traditional approaches to recommendation, in particular the need to model both the preferences of users and the properties of the items they consume. However, there are several aspects that make bartering problems interesting and challenging, specifically the fact that they are highly constrained, as only a limited vocabulary of items may be available to be traded at any given time. In this paper we propose new models for bartering-based recommendation. In particular, we show that existing methods (based on matching algorithms) do not perform well on real-world bartering data. Instead, we develop approaches based on Matrix Factorization and Factorization Machines, in order to model reciprocal interest between two users and each other's items. We evaluate our approach on three new datasets of trades, covering books, video games, and beers, where we obtain promising empirical performance compared to existing techniques, as well as serendipity in terms of the items being recommended.
